[CH1] Ten principles of Economics

Posted on 2024-09-07 In Economics
Concepts and principles

Concepts

  1. Scarcity: the limited nature of society's resources.
  2. Economics: the study of how society manages its scarce resources.
  3. Efficiency: the property of society getting the most it can from its scarce resources.
  4. Equality: the property of distributing economic prosperity uniformly among the members of society.
  5. Opportunity cost: whatever must be given up to obtain some item.
  6. Rational people: people who systematically and purposefully do the best they can to achive their objectives. Economists assume that people are rational (to illustrate their actions).
  7. Marginal change: an incremental adjustment to a plan of action.
  8. Incentive: sth inducing a person to act.
  9. Market economy: an economy that allocates resources through the decentralized decisions of many firms and households as they interact in markets for goods and services.
  10. Market failure: a situation in which a market left on its own does not allocate resources efficiently.
  11. Externality: the impact of one person's actions on the well-being of a bystander.
  12. Market power: the ability of a single economic actor(or small group of actors) to have a substantial influence on market prices.
  13. Productivity: the quantity of goods and services produced from each unit of labor input.
  14. Inflation: an increase in the overall level of prices in the economy.
  15. Business cycle: fluctuations in ecnonomic activity, sucha s employment and production.

The 10 Principles

Regarding how people make decisions

1. People Face trade-offs.

  • why decide

2. The cost of sth is what you give up to get it.

  • general criteria for the decision

3. Rational people think at the margin.

  • where to review (looking into the 'cost')

4. People respond to incentives.

  • when decide (Both direct and indirect effects should be carefully considered.)

Regarding how people interact with one another.

5. Trade can make everyone better off.

  • why trade/competition

6. Markets are usually a good way to organize economic activity.

  • why market
  • Prices are the instrument with which the 'invisible hand' directs economic activity: reflects both the sellers' costs of productoin and the value of the goods to the buyers
  • Also: guide market participants to reach outcomes that maximize the well-being of the society as a whole
    > Corollary : When a government prevents prices from adjusting to supply and demand, it impedes the invisible hand's ability to coordinate the decisions of the entities that make up an economy.

7. Governments can sometimes improve market outcomes.

Why we need government: proper role and scope

  • Enforcement of rules and maintenance of crucial institutions to market economy--most importantly propery rights

    • Making up for the invisible hand

      • Promoting efficiency: in case of market failure

        Probable reasons:

        • Externality ignored

        • Market power (e.g. monopoly)

      • Promoting equality

Regarding how the economy as a whole works

8. A country's standard of living depends on its ability to produce goods and services.

  • Key: productivity

9. Prices rise when the government prints too much money.

10. Society faces a short-run trade-off between inflation and unemployment.
